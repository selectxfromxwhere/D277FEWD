HELENA

Historic Helena MT

Helena is the capital of Montana, founded on October 30, 1864. Located in Lewis and Clark County, it sits at the southeastern part of the state at the foot of the Continental Divide. Helena is considered an urban micropolitan statistical area, having a population of just over 33,885 people.

Points Of Interest

Gates of the Mountains

Follow a scenic river through a canyon with towering cliffs. Located in the Lewis and Clark National Forest.

Cathedral of St. Helena

This Roman Catholic gothic cathedral was built in 1908, visitors can tour the stunning interior or attend mass.

Montana State Capitol

Constructed in 1902, the capital building features an impressive routunda, the Hall of Governors, and the Governor's Reception Room.
