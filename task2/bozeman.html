BOZEMAN

Bozeman

Bozeman is currently the fastest growing micropolitan area of the US, the tech and tourism industries have taken off in recent years. The city began as a rest stop for gold miners traversing the Oregon Trail and was founded in 1863. Located in Gallatin County, it sits at the southwestern part of the state in the Gallatin National Forest, near the entrance to Yellowstone National Park. Bozeman is considered an urban micropolitan statistical area, having a population of just over 56,123 people.
